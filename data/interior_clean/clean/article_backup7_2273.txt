Leaked Pentagon file reveals US will not step in to help Europe if Russia attacksThe US will not come to the aid of Europe if Russia invaded, a leaked Pentagon memo has revealed. By Max Parry, Lauren Peacock 22:51 ET, Mon, Mar 31, 2025 | UPDATED: 22:52 ET, Mon, Mar 31, 2025 The memo reveals the US will not intervene if Russia attacks any European allies A leaked Pentagon memo has shockingly revealed that the United States may not come to Europe's aid if Russia were to attack.US Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth stated in the document that Europe should not depend on the White House to send troops in the event of a Russian invasion.The 'Interim National Defense Strategic Guidance' memo, which was leaked, indicated that American soldiers needed for deterring China and ensuring the defense of US territory would not be dispatched to protect European allies.However, the document did note that the US would continue to support Europe with its nuclear deterrence. Mike Martin MP, a member of the UK's Defence Select Committee, told Express.co.uk that it is crucial for Europe to act swiftly to secure its safety, assuming it cannot rely on US resources."This leaked report tells us in black and white what we've known for six weeks: the US is not interested in the defence of the European continent. The UK now needs to work rapidly with our European allies to deliver collective security in Europe without the US", he said.The confidential document unveiled the Trump administration's near-total prioritisation of defending US interests against China. The report suggests that Europe must increase its defence investment to counter potential Russian aggression, especially in the absence of American intervention.According to the memo cited by the Washington Post, European security investment "will also ensure NATO can reliably deter or defeat Russian aggression even if deterrence fails and the United States is already engaged in, or must withhold forces to deter, a primary conflict in another region". Tobias Ellwood, the former chair of the Defence Select Committee, warned that the leaked memo could "embolden" the Kremlin "to strive ever further" into Europe."It's a wake-up call to Europe and follows a pattern of evidence that America is bizarrely choosing to get closer to Russia - seeing the world in a very different perspective than any American predecessor since the war."Europe quite rightly can no longer see our closest security ally as a guarantor if Russia continues pursuing its own objective expanding westwards", cautioned the former Conservative MP for Bournemouth East."This is again, unfortunately, confirms the entire new real politik", he said, adding: "It will embolden, it will enhance, and it will encourage Russia to strive ever further [into Europe]. "Now that Trump is giving every indication that it's not got Europe's back. It's made Europe all the more dangerous. This is confirmation if we needed it already that we've moved into a new era of insecurity and we've got to shape up."
